# Solar System Star Planets
1 The smallest and fastest planet, Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and whips around it every 88 Earth days. SUN Mercury
2 Spinning in the opposite direction to most planets, Venus is the hottest planet, and one of the brightest objects in the sky. SUN Venus
3 The place we call home, Earth is the third rock from the sun and the only planet with known life on it - and lots of it too! SUN Earth
4 The red planet is dusty, cold world with a thin atmosphere and is home to four NASA robots. SUN Mars
5 Jupiter is a massive planet, twice the size of all other planets combined, and has a centuries-old storm that is bigger than Earth. SUN Jupiter
6 The most recognizable planet with a system of icy rings, Saturn is a very unique and interesting planet. SUN Saturn
7 Uranus has a very unique rotation--it spins on its side at an almost 90-degree angle, unlike other planets. SUN Uranus
8 Neptune is now the most distant planet and is a cold and dark world nearly 3 billion miles from the Sun. SUN Neptune
9 Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt but was reclassified a dwarf planet in 2006 - even though it's 14 times smaller than Pluto. SUN Ceres
10 Pluto will always be the ninth planet to us! Smaller than Earth's moon, Pluto was a planet up until 2006 and has five of its own moons! SUN Pluto
11 Haumea lives in the Kuiper belt and is about the same size as Pluto. It spins very fast, which distorts its shape, making it look like a football. SUN Haumea
